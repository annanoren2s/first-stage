Advertising restrictions for policy violations

If we find that an advertiser doesn’t follow our Advertising Standards or other policies and terms, they may face advertising restrictions. The restrictions may be applied on an advertiser’s business account, ad account, page or user account:

  • Business account: A type of account that connects and centralises business assets, such as Facebook Pages, Instagram accounts, ad accounts and product catalogues, across the Meta ecosystem so that people can manage these assets together as a unified online business.
  • Ad account: An account used for managing ads across Meta technologies, which allows advertisers to create ads and advertising campaigns, pay for ads and see insights and analytics.
  • Page: A product that lets people and businesses create a presence and connect with the community. Every advertiser will need to have a Page created before they can advertise.

When a business account, ad account, page or user account is restricted, an advertiser won’t be able to use it to advertise across our technologies. For example, if an advertiser has a restricted page, they won’t be able to run ads associated with that page. Note that if a user account is restricted from advertising on a business account or ad account, other members of those accounts may still be able to advertise.
